GARBAGE CLASSIFICATION

About

Problem Statement

Garbage classification is a significant challenge in waste management. It involves categorizing waste items into different classes, such as recyclable, non-recyclable, organic, and hazardous materials. Traditional methods for waste sorting are often manual, time-consuming, and prone to human errors. To improve waste sorting efficiency and recycling rates, a deep learning-based approach can be employed to automatically classify garbage items into appropriate categories

Solution

This paper presents a simple yet effective solution for the garbage classification problem using a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) based approach. The methodology involves data collection of labeled garbage images, data preprocessing through augmentation and normalization, and the design of a CNN architecture. The model is trained and evaluated on a diverse dataset, demonstrating its accuracy and performance in waste item classification. Once successful, the model can be deployed in real-world applications such as waste sorting systems or smart waste bins. Continuous improvement is emphasized through periodic retraining and user feedback, ensuring adaptability to changing waste patterns. The proposed approach offers a promising and practical solution for automated garbage classification in waste management systems.
